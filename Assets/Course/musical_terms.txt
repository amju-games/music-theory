A tempo=@@@Back to normal speed
Accelerando=@@@Gradually faster
Adagio=@@@Slowly
Allegro=@@@Lively, quite fast
Allegretto=@@@Nearly as fast as Allegro
Andante=@@@Walking pace
Andantino=@@@A bit faster or slower than Andante
Cantabile=@@@Singing
Con=@@@With
Crescendo (cresc.)=@@@Gradually louder
Da capo (D.C.)=@@@From the beginning

Dolce=@@@Sweetly
Fine=@@@The end
Forte (f)=@@@Loud
Fortissimo (ff)=@@@Very loud
Forzando (fz)=@@@With a strong accent
Largo=@@@Slow and stately
Legato=@@@Smoothly
Leggiero=@@@Lightly
Lento=@@@Slowly
Maestoso=@@@Majestically
Mezzo forte (mf)=@@@Moderately loud
Mezzo piano (mp)=@@@Moderately soft
Moderato=@@@Moderate pace
Pesante=@@@Heavily
Piano (p)=@@@Soft
Pianissimo (pp)=@@@Very soft
Poco=@@@A little
Presto=@@@Very quick
Prestissimo=@@@As fast as possible
Rallentando (rall.)=@@@Gradually slower
Ritardando (ritard., rit.)=@@@Gradually slower
Ritenuto (riten., rit)=@@@Slower at once
Scherzo=@@@A joke
Scherzando=@@@Playfully
Sforzando (sf, sfz)=@@@With a sudden accent
Staccato=@@@Short, detached
Subito=@@@Suddenly
Tempo=@@@Speed
Tranquillo=@@@Quietly
Vivace=@@@Lively, quickly
